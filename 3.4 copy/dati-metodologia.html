Data and Methodology

The dataset we used is the Colonial Dates Dataset (COLDAT), an archive that collects detailed information on the extent and duration of European colonial empires.

In particular, for each currently independent country and its former overseas colonies, the dataset records the starting and ending years of colonization by the major colonial powers, based on standardized criteria and reputable secondary sources.

The eight powers considered are Belgium, Britain, France, Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Portugal, and Spain.

Thanks to these data, it is possible to systematically analyze periods of expansion and decline of colonialism in a comparative perspective, for example, by examining how long a given country remained colonized, by which power, and during which historical period.

To create the visualizations and the website, we conducted an in-depth analysis of the dataset, carefully examining all the information it contains and organizing it using pivot tables. We then transformed these data into graphical visualizations designed to represent the phenomenon of colonization as effectively as possible in all its aspects, without overlooking any relevant information contained in the dataset.
