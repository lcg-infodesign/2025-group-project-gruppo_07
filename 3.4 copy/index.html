The Bubble of colonialism

Exploration of European colonial powers

Colonialism was a historical and political process in which states expanded their control over foreign lands from the 15th to the 20th century.
Driven by the search for resources, markets, and global influence, colonial powers imposed their rule by force and reshaped local systems.
These actions deeply affected the development and identities of colonized societies. Their long-lasting consequences still produce inequalities and tensions today.

The website illustrates the phenomenon of colonialism over the period between 1450 and 2000. Thanks to an interactive timeline, users can thoroughly explore the historical evolution of colonialism and analyze the correlations between major colonial events and their temporal context.
In this way, the user is able to visually grasp the moments of peaks and declines in colonization.

Each static bubble represents a colonialist nation

while the abstract dots represent the colonized countries,
whose size is linked to the duration of their colonization.
